# VIT Bhopal — Hostel Rules & Procedures

## Hostel Structure
VIT Bhopal is a fully residential campus -- all first-year students stay in campus hostels. Boys are housed across Block 1 (8-storey) and Blocks 2-8 (including newer G+7 buildings); girls are housed in Block I and Block II (6-storey buildings). Room options range from single-seater to 8-bedded rooms, with both AC and non-AC choices.

## Hostel Fees
Hostel fees (covering 10 months, room rent and mess) vary by block and room type, roughly ranging from about Rs. 1,19,000 per year for the most economical 8-bedded non-AC room up to around Rs. 2,08,000 per year for AC rooms in premium blocks. In addition to the room fee, new hostel joiners pay a one-time non-refundable admission fee of Rs. 17,500 and a refundable caution deposit of Rs. 15,000. Exact current rates by block and room type are published on the official VIT Bhopal hostel fee structure page each year, since rates are revised annually.

## Mess
Meals are served through the hostel mess, typically covering breakfast, lunch, snacks, and dinner, with both vegetarian and non-vegetarian options and a rotating monthly menu. Breakfast service generally starts around 7:00 AM, with dinner service running through the evening into the night.

## Leaving Campus / Curfew
Hostellers must take prior permission from the Warden before leaving campus, including for overnight outings. Leave requests are submitted through the VTOP portal. VIT Bhopal uses computerized biometric attendance to track hostel residents, including night attendance checks. Students are generally expected back on campus by a fixed evening cutoff unless they have prior outing permission.

## Visitor Policy
No outsider is allowed to stay inside the hostel, and visitors are not permitted overnight accommodation under any circumstance.

## Prohibited Activities
Gambling, possession of firearms, and ragging are strictly prohibited within the hostel and attract disciplinary action, including possible suspension or expulsion. Smoking, alcohol, and drugs are also banned inside hostel premises.

## Medical Support
Hostels have round-the-clock access to a medical centre, ambulance services, and on-call doctors for emergencies.

## Room Allotment
Room allotment happens through VTOP during the hostel booking window, where students select their block, room type, and mess caterer preference. Allotment is often influenced by VITEEE rank or academic performance (CGPA) in later years.

## Other Facilities
Hostel blocks typically include Wi-Fi, a gym, indoor/outdoor sports courts, an ATM, a pharmacy, and laundry services.
